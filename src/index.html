Welcome one and all to our brief overview of the Internet of Things!

So, what is the Internet of Things (IoT)? Well! It's the idea that ALL devices have the ability to talk to each other through an Internet Connection. By all, we really do mean ALL. Fridges, Watches, Keys, Cookers, Worktops. Everything.

However! The IoT is a rather old concept. While it was named back in 1999, it only truly gained interest in 2005! But interestingly enough, it all started way back in 1832..

This website was created to give the future generations, aged 10 and above an idea on where the Internet of Things came from and where it may be going; alongside any issues the IoT presents us

Welcome to our Overview of the Internet of Things!

1832

A small machine called the "telegraph" was made by Baron Schilling to let two people talk to each other that lived far away from each other. It allowed you to talk to each other through beeps!

1969

The barcode was invented by Norman Joseph when he drew some squiggly lines in the sand! Shop workers now could scan in your shopping and get a price from it!

1980

The Internet is born! Students from the University of California could now communicate with other universities through the internet! It'd be a while before they all had Facebook accounts though...

1993

The World's FIRST Webcam was made - all to keep an eye on a pot of coffee! Whilst this happened, the world's first SMARTPHONE was made.. though it was nothing like the smartphones of today

2000

LG released the World's FIRST internet-connected fridge. It could track the contents of your fridge and also automatically order more when something was close to running out!

2008/ 2009

The Internet of Things is BORN! The amount of internet-connected devices surpasses the world population!!

Smart Motorway

Sensors installed on Smart Motorways are able to detect the amount of vehicles current on the road and adjust the speed limit so to help reduce collisions!

Stock Management

Big supermarkets use a system called EPOS (Electronic Point of Sales) to track the amount of stock they have and whenever anything is bought. This allows the supermarket to order in more products as and when then need to.

Home Security

Sensors around the windows and doors can detect when they are forced open. Police encourage owning these devices to cut down on burglars. The IOT has allowed for you to keep all your toys and super cool gadgets safe and sound...

Patient Monitoring

So.. ya know those monitors in hospitals that go beep, beep, beep? That is is but one of many pieces of equipment that hospitals use monitor lots of vital signs of a patient including blood pressure to even your brain activity! These machines have helped save millions of lives and continue to get better and more accurate.

Smart Home Meters

Energy companies are currently pushing these out to all homes. These devices track how much electricity certain rooms or devices are using so you can cut down on things that use a lot of electricity. That is.. as long as noone unplugs your console!

Animal Tracking

Farmers cant have their cows and sheep escaping, there not all like Shaun the Sheep. Sensors inside farm animals, allow farmers to track the position of their animals when they are out grazing in the field.

Smart Parking

Designed by Freepik

Sensors in Car Parks can tell how many cars are parked in a Car Park. They can then send this data to the entrances to Town Centres and shopping centres so the General Public can see if theres any spare parking spots.
