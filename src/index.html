The History of the

Internet of Things!

A small machine called the "telegraph" was made by Baron Schilling to let two people talk to each other that lived far away from each other. It allowed you to talk to each other through beeps!

The barcode was invented by Norman Joseph when he drew some squiggly lines in the sand! Shop workers now could scan in your shopping and get a price from it!

The Internet is born! Students from the University of California could now communicate with students from other universities through the internet. It would be a while before they all had Facebook accounts though...

Some students from Carnegie-Mellon University installed a Vending Machine that could count how many drinks it had and tell staff when it was running out. Now they would never run out of Coca Cola!

The first webcam was made to keep an eye on a coffee pot! I’d rather have tea to be honest.. Oh… they also made the World’s FIRST Smartphone :) Though it was a tad different from the Mobiles of today.

LG have released the first fridges that can be connected to the Internet. You could track how much food you had left in the fridge and order more over the internet whenever you were running low. You could never run out of chocolate this way.

Only in 2002 was the term “The Internet of Things” first used. The Internet of Things finally has a name…only took 170 years!
